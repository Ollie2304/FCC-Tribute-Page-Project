Sir William Drummond Macdonald Paton

Sir William Drummond Macdonald Paton was a British pharmacologist who was born 5th May 1917 and died 17th October 1993.
And he was awarded three awards during his time as a pharmacologist:

  • Cameron Prize for Therapeutics of the University of Edinburgh (1956)
  • Gairdner Foundation International Award (1959)

Paton discovered two new classes of drugs which acted on nicotinic acetylcholine receptors which layed down the foundation for the development of Decamethonium which was the first neuromuscular blocking drug that was specific. In addition, this discovery also helped develop Hexamethonium which was the first drug to specifically and safely lower blood pressure.
